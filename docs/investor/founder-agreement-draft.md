# Founder Agreement (Draft)

Last updated: 2026-05-23
Status: Draft for internal alignment (not legal advice)

## 1. Parties
This Founder Agreement ("Agreement") is entered by the founders listed in Appendix A (each a "Founder", collectively the "Founders") for the operation of Vibe Coding (the "Company").

## 2. Purpose
The Founders agree to:
- define ownership and vesting mechanics,
- assign intellectual property rights,
- establish decision-making rules,
- define commitment standards and exit handling.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities
Each Founder is assigned a primary function and quarterly objectives.

- Founder A (CEO): product strategy, fundraising, key partnerships.
- Founder B (CTO): platform architecture, security, data integrity, engineering delivery.
- Founder C (Learning/Curriculum): content quality, tutor enablement, learning outcomes.
- Optional Founder D (Growth): user acquisition, channel strategy, conversion optimization.

Detailed role scope and KPI definitions are maintained in a separate operating memo and reviewed quarterly.

## 4. Equity and Vesting
### 4.1 Equity Allocation
Initial share allocation is defined in Appendix B (cap table) and approved unanimously by Founders.

### 4.2 Vesting Standard
Unless otherwise unanimously agreed in writing:
- Vesting term: 48 months.
- Cliff: 12 months.
- Vesting frequency after cliff: monthly.
- Unvested shares are subject to repurchase by Company at nominal value upon termination.

### 4.3 Founder Departure
- Good Leaver: resignation for approved reasons, disability, death, or mutually agreed departure.
- Bad Leaver: fraud, gross misconduct, material breach, or abandonment.
- Treatment of vested/unvested shares follows board-approved leaver policy and applicable law.

## 5. Intellectual Property (IP)
### 5.1 Assignment
All inventions, source code, architecture, curriculum artifacts, domain names, trademarks, and data models created for Company business are assigned to the Company.

### 5.2 Prior IP
Any pre-existing IP used in Company products must be disclosed and licensed to Company in writing with explicit terms.

### 5.3 Open Source and Third-party Assets
Founders must ensure third-party licenses are compatible with Company use and distribution.

## 6. Confidentiality and Non-Solicitation
- Founders must keep Company confidential information private during and after service.
- Founders agree not to solicit Company employees/contractors for a period defined by applicable local law.

## 7. Time Commitment
Each Founder agrees to the commitment level in Appendix A.
- Full-time founders prioritize Company over external engagements.
- Part-time founders must disclose all material external commitments.

## 8. Decision Rights and Governance
### 8.1 Reserved Matters (Unanimous Founder Approval)
The following require unanimous written approval:
- issuing new shares beyond approved option pool,
- merger, acquisition, liquidation,
- sale of core IP,
- debt/guarantees above threshold,
- amendment of this Agreement.

### 8.2 Board/Founder Voting
Until formal board expansion:
- ordinary operating decisions: simple majority,
- strategic decisions (budget, senior hiring, pricing model changes): supermajority (>= 67%).

## 9. Compensation and Reimbursement
- Founder salary policy is tied to runway and board-approved budget.
- Expense reimbursement requires receipts and policy compliance.

## 10. Deadlock Resolution
If Founders cannot resolve a reserved matter:
1. Internal mediation period (14 days).
2. External mediator jointly appointed.
3. If unresolved, arbitration/litigation venue per governing law clause.

## 11. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law
This Agreement is governed by the law specified in Appendix C.
Disputes shall be resolved according to the dispute mechanism in Appendix C.

## 12. Term and Amendments
- Effective on signature date.
- Amendments require unanimous written consent.
